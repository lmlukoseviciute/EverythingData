A/B Testing of Fast Food Marketing Strategies

This dataset compares the effectiveness of different marketing strategies through A/B testing in the fast food industry.

Overview

The new product was introduced at multiple randomly selected locations, with each location assigned one of the three promotional strategies. Weekly sales data for the new item were collected over a four-week period to measure the performance of each campaign. This Kaggle dataset captures sales data from a fast-food chain's A/B testing of three different marketing campaigns over four weeks. It includes information on store locations, market characteristics, promotional strategies, and sales performance.

  • A Kruskal-Wallis H test indicated significant differences in sales between at least one pair of promotional groups. Follow-up Mann-Whitney U tests revealed that Promotions 1 and 3 performed best, while Promotion 2 was significantly worse in terms of generated sales.
  • Although the median sales for Promotion 1 (55.39) was slightly higher than Promotion 3 (51.16), the difference was not statistically significant. Therefore, other considerations—such as implementation cost, operational feasibility, or scalability—should factor into the final decision between these top-performing campaigns.
