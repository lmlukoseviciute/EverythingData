A/B Testing of Cookie Cats Game Update Strategies

This dataset captures player behavior before and after game update changes in the mobile game Cookie Cats. Insights in different update strategies through A/B testing.

Overview

This analysis explores the impact of a game design change in Cookie Cats a popular mobile puzzle game. Specifically, it examines the effect of shifting the game's first content gate from level 30 to level 40 on player engagement and retention. To assess this, an A/B test was conducted involving 90,189 new players, who were randomly assigned to one of two groups: a control group with the gate at level 30 (gate_30) and a test group with the gate at level 40 (gate_40).

Key Highlights

There was a statistically significant difference in 7-day retention between the groups. However:

  • The risk difference was only 0.008, meaning 0.8% more users were retained in gate_30 than in gate_40.
  • This small effect size could be:
    • A real but minor impact of gate placement
    • A result of bias introduced by SRM
    • Or a behavioral artifact â€” players may have lost interest earlier than encountering level 40.
