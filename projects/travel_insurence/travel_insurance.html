Travel Insurance Prediction with Supervised ML

Exploratory data analysis and statistical hypotheses testing of travel insurence data. Predicting insurence outcomes with suppervised machine learning algorithms.

Overview

This project analyzes a Kaggle dataset from India collected in 2017–2019 to predict whether a customer will purchase travel insurance using supervised machine learning. The data includes demographic and behavioral attributes like age, travel history, and employment type. Also two hypotheses were tested: insurance buyers have higher incomes, and individuals who have traveled abroad earn more than those who haven’t.

  • Hypotheses testing using Mann–Whitney U tests and Kolmogorov–Smirnov tests showed that customers who bought travel insurance tend to have higher annual income than those who did not. Also customers who had ever travel tend to have higher annual income than those who did not.
  • Trained Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Support Vector Machine models. After evaluating all models, the top 3 performers based on F1 score were combined into a Voting Classifier.
